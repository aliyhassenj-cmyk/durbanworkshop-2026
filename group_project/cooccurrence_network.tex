\subsection{Overview}\label{overview}

This network visualises co-occurrence relationships in the diagnostic
accuracy literature on scabies (\emph{Sarcoptes scabiei}). Each edge
represents a study that links S. scabiei to a host, a diagnostic method,
a sample type, or an antigen type. The thicker the edge, the more
studies support that link.

\subsection{Interpretation}\label{interpretation}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Sarcoptes scabiei} (orange, centre) connects to all other
  nodes.
\item
  \textbf{Hosts:} 16 studies on humans (teal), 37 on animals (brown ---
  pigs, rabbits, dogs, foxes, sheep, chamois, bears).
\item
  \textbf{Molecular diagnostics} (blues): qPCR/cPCR (8 studies), LAMP
  (1), RT-qPCR (2).
\item
  \textbf{Immunological diagnostics} (green): ELISA dominates with 42
  studies. Nearly all ELISA studies used \textbf{serum} (dashed green
  edge) --- serum acts as a \emph{moderator} variable between the
  diagnostic method and the pathogen.
\item
  \textbf{Clinical/morphological} (reds): Skin scrapings and swabs are
  the sample collection methods used across both diagnostic approaches.
\item
  \textbf{Antigen types} (golds/purple): Whole antigen (16), recombinant
  (23), DNA target (11).
\item
  Edge thickness reflects the number of linking studies --- more
  evidence behind that connection.
\end{itemize}
